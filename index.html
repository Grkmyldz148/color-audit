The Open-Source Color Audit

We measured the color scales everyone copies.

Six design systems. 24 scales. 252 tokens. One perceptual ruler.

Every hex token from Tailwind, Primer, Material, Bootstrap, Radix and Chakra was mapped into helmlab GenSpace and scored on step uniformity, lightness monotonicity, hue drift and WCAG contrast — measured, not guessed. Read the methodology.

Radix Colors

Uneven by explicit design

@radix-ui/colors@3.0.0 (light scales)

17.2°worst hue drift

Caveat: Radix is role-based by design. Its documentation assigns each of the 12 steps a UI role rather than promising even perceptual spacing, so step-uniformity metrics measure a contract Radix never signed. The rank below is only meaningful if you need an even ramp.

bluestep CV 92.7% · max/min 11.8× · hue drift 17.2°
redstep CV 100.0% · max/min 14.1× · hue drift 11.2°
graystep CV 143.5% · max/min 73.9× · hue drift — (achromatic)

Bars: perceptual distance between adjacent tokens (helmlab GenSpace). Even bars = even scale. All bars share one absolute vertical scale across every system.

  • Raw numbers are the audit's highest (mean step CV 101.6%, gray max/min 73.9×) — but Radix documents its 12 steps as use-case roles (1–2 backgrounds, 3–5 component states, 9 solid, 11–12 text), not an even ramp. The big 11→12 text jumps (red: 0.435, the largest single step in the audit) are intentional.
  • Re-scored on interior steps 2–11 only, blue and green become respectable (step CV 30.8% / 37.3%); red stays uneven (64.6%) and gray remains at 97.6% even without the role endpoints.
  • Within its own logic it is consistent: solid step 9 always lands mid-contrast and lightness never reverses. Judged as a perceptual ramp it ranks last; judged as a role system it is coherent — weigh which contract you need.

Methodology

Every hex token is mapped into helmlab GenSpace (hl.genFromHex(hex) → [L, a, b] with L in 0–1; hl.genToLch → [L, C, h°]), a perceptually uniform generation space. Four measurements per scale:

1 · Step-distance consistency (step CV)

d_i = GenSpace Euclidean distance(token_i, token_{i+1}) step CV = std(d) / mean(d) × 100 (plus max/min ratio)

Lower CV = more even perceptual steps. This is what the bar charts on each card show.

2 · Lightness uniformity (L-CV) and monotonicity

dL_i = L_{i+1} − L_i L-CV = std(dL) / |mean(dL)| × 100 (any sign flip → monotonicity violation)

3 · Hue drift

drift = max_i |h_i − h_first| with 360° wrap, skipping steps with C < 0.03 as achromatic

4 · Contrast

WCAG contrastRatio(mid, #ffffff) and contrastRatio(mid, #000000) flag if both < 4.5 (mid = 500, Radix step 9, Primer step 5)

Provenance & limitations

  • Token sources are the official npm packages, versions recorded in data/tokens/*.json. Tailwind v4 publishes oklch(), converted to sRGB hex via culori; Bootstrap's chromatic ramps are generated exactly per its own tint-color/shade-color (sass mix()) definitions.
  • This audit measures scale quality only — how evenly and predictably a ramp is spaced in a perceptual space. It says nothing about overall design quality, aesthetics, component design, or the fitness of these palettes for their intended workflows.
  • Radix Colors is role-based by design: its 12 steps are documented use-case roles, not an even ramp, so uniformity metrics measure a contract it never signed. This caveat is repeated on its card.
  • Results depend on the choice of perceptual space; GenSpace correlates strongly with OKLab-class spaces, so orderings should be broadly stable, but exact numbers are space-specific.
